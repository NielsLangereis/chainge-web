track record

Every prediction lands in a public ledger.

This is the methodology track record. Every cascade prediction Chainge ships carries a locked horizon, capped at 90 days. When the horizon arrives, the prediction is scored against primary-source data: right, partial, wrong, or unclear. The original text is never edited. The ledger reads as a record, not a story.

Two states travel together on every row. The prediction window has its own lifecycle (pending, then closed at the horizon), and the underlying situation has its own (active while the event runs, resolved when it ends). A closed window does not mean a resolved situation. Hormuz is the live case: the first prediction window closed on June 1, the strait situation runs on.

02 · how to read a verdict

The receipts here, the method there.

A pending window means the horizon has not arrived. A closed window carries a verdict: right, partial, wrong, or unclear, scored mechanically against primary-source data where the data exists, and against proxy or operator evidence where it does not, with that path disclosed. Some terminal claims (private-SME margin compression, for one) have no public falsifiable observable; the ledger says so rather than scoring them silently.

For how cascades get generated, how the harness grounds claims, and how verdicts get scored, see how it works. The track-record page is the receipts; how-it-works is the method.
