01

How to read this packet

Read each cascade as a short thesis on your regulated economics. Three parts: a catalyst (the event or rule change), a transmission path (how it reaches Westland Infra), and an impact (where it lands, your regulated revenue, your congestion economics, or your capital base). Two things we add on top.

First, a conviction number from 0 to 1. It is our conviction, not a market-implied probability, so read 0.48 as a stronger anchor than 0.30, not as a 48 percent chance. This week 0.48 is our highest-conviction call. A chain is either a headline (clears our bar to be a lead call this week) or watch-tier (on the radar, not yet strong enough to act on).

Second, every link is tagged grounded or estimated. Grounded means it rests on something hard we hold: a price series, a legal text, a public register, your own network data. Estimated means we reasoned it from your profile. When a chain leans on something we cannot evidence, an internal skeptic check marks it down, which this week is why the two regulatory-timing chains sit at watch.

So what

Directive 2024/1788 gives the Netherlands a legal basis it did not previously have to set a binding gas phase-out date for heating, including greenhouse clusters, likely by the late 2020s. If and when a firm end-date lands, it pressures the depreciation pathway and cost-recovery treatment on your regulated gas distribution asset base. It stays low at 0.14 because the Dutch schedule is not set, and we hold the chain at that open node rather than pricing a date that does not yet exist.

Read the numbers as ordinal calibration, not market odds: 0.48 is a stronger anchor than 0.30, and the two chains that eased did so because the engine refuses to price an open regulatory date.
