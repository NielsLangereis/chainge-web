Update read

This iteration moves in two directions, not one. Two of your clusters moderated and one strengthened, and the reason each moved is the methodology point worth sitting with. Cluster B eases from 0.32 to 0.18 and Cluster C from 0.42 to 0.18, because the harness now declines to carry probability that rests on EUR-Lex text it cannot read. Cascade A rises from 0.28 to 0.35, because the chain was re-grounded: its probability now rests on sustained energy-cost observables and an explicit model of cost persistence, rather than on a single read of whether the Strait reopens by one date.

That re-grounding is the substantive change. The earlier framing leaned on the Hormuz de-escalation question as if a reopening would switch the cascade off. It would not. The binding constraint sits downstream, in your ACM-revenue exposure, and the cost that drives it decays on its own lagged path whatever the geopolitics do. Hormuz is the gravity event that frames the cascade; it is no longer the floor under it.

The cleaner read of the week is that the engine moved each cascade in the direction its grounding pointed: down where the evidence thinned, up where it strengthened. That is what a calibration loop is supposed to do. A matrix that only ever drifts one way is not calibrating; this one discriminated.
