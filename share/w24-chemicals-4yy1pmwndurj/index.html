First read

This is the first run scoped to your profile, so one paragraph on the method before the predictions. A cascade is a traced chain of cause and effect that starts at a public event and ends at a specific exposure on your business: each step names a mechanism, carries its own source grounding, and resolves at a stated horizon. The harness is the data layer underneath: this week it reaches Italian energy and environmental sources for the first time (ARERA tariff filings, Snam network transparency data, the European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register), alongside the Chinese regulatory layer and the EU chemicals register. A probability here is ordinal calibration of the chain, not a point forecast; read 0.34 as a stronger anchor than 0.28, not as a market price. The early nodes are anchored on public data; the terminal nodes are anchored on your profile, which is why some of them resolve against a public register and some need your judgment at horizon. Three cascades follow, each a new cluster created on this run.

Cascade A in detail

Italian gas market to Italian production cost base. This is one of the first cascades to run on the Phase 1c Italian harness layer: ARERA tariff filings, Snam network transparency data, and European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register sibling-site records.

Step 1

Italian gas market tightens into the injection season.

ARERA monthly tariff revisions and Snam storage fill-rate reporting set the gas-cost signal for Italian industrial users. Storage trajectory ahead of the November 2026 injection-season close is the load-bearing observable; a shortfall against the seasonal fill path holds the wholesale price elevated. Node grounding is public Italian regulator and transmission-operator data.

Step 2

Italian power prices track gas at the margin.

Gas-fired plant sets the marginal clearing price across much of the Italian electricity market, so an elevated gas curve passes through into industrial power tariffs. Energy-curing resin processing and photoinitiator synthesis are both electricity- and heat-intensive, which is where the transmission becomes specific to your production rather than generic to Italian industry.

Step 3

The Italian-produced cost base rises.

The combined gas and power signal lands on the cost base of your Italian-produced photoinitiator and energy-curing resins product family. European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register sibling-site records confirm the energy and emissions intensity of comparable Italian specialty-chemicals sites, giving the intensity assumption a public anchor. The size of the pass-through is a site-level question, not a market one.

Step 4

Margin compresses unless the increase is passed on.

An input-cost increase on the Italian lines compresses margin unless it is recovered through OEM pricing, and pricing power varies by customer and by competitive position against non-Italian production. The relative weights here are reasoning from the profile, not yet from per-account contract data.

Step 5

Terminal: a cost shift visible in management accounts.

The exposure terminates at a private observable: a movement in the Italian-site cost base that shows up in internal reporting, not in any public dataset. The verdict path is operator judgment at horizon, with the ARERA Q3 publications and the Snam fill-rate reports as the public markers that bracket the window.

Methodology note Five prediction checks landed on this chain, concentrated on nodes 1 and 2 where the public Italian sources reach. The Italian grounding stack (ARERA, Snam, European Pollutant Release and Transfer Register) is new in the harness this iteration; yours is the first profile in the matrix to require it. The downstream nodes carry profile-anchored reasoning rather than per-component primary data, which is the honest read on where the chain is strong and where it is still an estimate.

Cascade B in detail

Chinese coal grid and environmental regulation to Chinese production cost base, and onward to the photoinitiator intermediate-supply network dependent on those plants.

Step 1

Provincial energy-intensity targets and enforcement tighten.

The producing province's 15th Five-Year-Plan energy-intensity reduction targets, together with Ministry of Ecology and Environment enforcement campaigns for Yangtze Economic Belt chemical parks, set the policy pressure on energy use and emissions at park level. Both are public publications, which is where the chain has its firmest grounding.

Step 2

Energy and compliance costs rise for park operators.

Coal-grid power cost, energy-intensity dual-control pressure, and environmental-compliance retrofit obligations raise the operating cost of chemical-park production. During active enforcement windows the constraint can move from cost to availability, with curtailment rather than a price signal as the binding effect.

Step 3

Your Chinese plant-level cost base moves.

The park-level pressure lands on your Chinese production operations as higher energy and compliance cost, with possible production curtailment during enforcement campaigns. This is the node where the public data thins out, since firm-level granularity for a specialty-chemicals SME is not in the reachable layer (see below).

Step 4

The intermediate-supply network transmits the shock.

Where those plants feed photoinitiator intermediates into the global network, a cost or availability shock does not stay local; it ripples to downstream photoinitiator synthesis wherever it depends on that intermediate supply. The dependency structure is the mechanism that turns a Chinese provincial policy into a cost question for the wider product family.

Step 5

Terminal: a private cost shift, with public secondary markers.

The exposure terminates at a private cost observable, with the verdict path through operator judgment. Secondary public markers exist: a customer communication, a force-majeure notice, or an investor-update reference to operational constraints would each be observable signals that the constraint has bound.

Honest gap The Chinese firm-level data layer is the open gap on this cascade. The harness reaches public Ministry of Ecology and Environment enforcement announcements, provincial Five-Year-Plan publications, and parent-firm KPIs for listed competitors (for example Jiuri New Material, SH:688199; Yangfan New Materials, SZ:300637). It does not yet reach firm-level granularity for a Chinese specialty-chemicals SME. The cascade is therefore well grounded at the policy layer and at the listed-competitor layer, and explicitly estimated at the node that touches your plant directly.
05

Cascade C in detail

EU REACH Substance of Very High Concern process to photoinitiator revenue pressure. This is the methodology bright spot in the packet: the only cascade whose verdict resolves mechanically against a public register.

Step 1

A photoinitiator chemistry enters the REACH pipeline.

Under REACH (Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006, CELEX:32006R1907), a member-state or ECHA proposal can place a substance into the Registry of Intentions as a candidate Substance of Very High Concern. The observable at this node is the presence or absence of a photoinitiator-chemistry dossier in that public register.

Step 2

Candidate List inclusion triggers obligations.

Inclusion on the REACH Candidate List, decided at the biannual update, carries communication and, on subsequent steps, authorisation obligations down the supply chain. The next ECHA Candidate List update (December 2026 or June 2027) is the resolution point for whether this node fires.

Step 3

OEM customers begin supplier-qualification review.

EU ink and coating OEM customers respond to an SVHC signal by opening reformulation and supplier-qualification review, often ahead of any hard restriction. This step is observable in trade press, for example European Coatings Journal reporting, typically within three to six months of a listing.

Step 4

Revenue pressure concentrates on the EU accounts.

The pressure lands on your photoinitiator product family, the dominant share of group revenue, and concentrates on EU OEM ink and coating accounts where the REACH precedent applies most directly. Substitution and requalification risk is the transmission mechanism from a register entry to an account-level revenue effect.

Step 5

Terminal: a mechanically resolvable verdict.

The terminal claim resolves against the public ECHA register: a photoinitiator dossier in the Registry of Intentions or on the Candidate List either appears or it does not. No operator judgment is required to mark this one. This is the cascade in the packet that the matrix can score on its own.

Mechanical resolution Four prediction checks landed on this chain, on nodes 1, 2, and 5. Cascade C is the only W24 cascade with a terminal observable that is public and falsifiable. At horizon the matrix reads the ECHA register and assigns the verdict directly, without asking you to characterise the outcome. That is the methodology working at full strength: a chain that states a precise, dated, register-checkable claim and then waits to be marked against it.

How verdicts register

Matrix verdict vocabulary At the verdict horizon, each prediction resolves to one of right / partial / wrong / unclear. Two paths feed that resolution. Cascade C resolves mechanically: the matrix reads the public ECHA register and marks it without your input. Cascades A and B resolve through an operator-feedback path, because their terminal claims sit on private observables that no public dataset exposes; your read at horizon is the verdict signal. No verdict is asked of you today. This section is here so the vocabulary is familiar when the horizons arrive, and so the distinction between the two resolution paths is on the table from the first share.
