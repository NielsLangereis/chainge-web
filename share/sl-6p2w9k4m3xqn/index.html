03 · coverage

What it covers, and what it leaves out

The line covers only cascades that terminate in a macro, commodity, FX, or rate instrument with a clean public price. Operator-margin, compliance, and regulatory cascades have no single tradeable instrument and are not scored here. Of 26 tradeable candidates, 20 locked cleanly and 6 were excluded, for the honest reasons below.

  • Defence-surge cascade (2026-06-22): no public price observation on or before the surface date.
  • CBAM chemicals cascade (2026-06-15): the marker tests a spread or ratio, with no single instrument to lock.
  • Rare-earth FX cascade (2026-06-15): compound terminus, no single instrument to lock.
  • SME capex cascade (2026-06-15): no public price observation on or before the surface date.
  • Health-emergency cobalt cascade (2026-06-01): no public price observation on or before the surface date.
  • US-power into EU ammonia cascade (2026-05-18): compound terminus, no single instrument to lock.
04 · method

How a call is locked and marked

The reference is the public series print on or before the surface date, so the entry is leak-free and reproducible. A call marks right or wrong at its horizon, or to date on a still-open window, against that series. To-date marks on open windows count toward the headline; a resolved-only count is kept separately, and is currently zero because no horizon has elapsed. Series used include EU natural gas, Brent crude, EUR/USD, KRW/USD, and US producer prices, each a public reference.

The illustrative basket is equal-weight and can be dominated by a single large move, which is one reason the hit-rate, not the basket, is the load-bearing number. The basket return is simulated, hypothetical, and shown to no backer or reader until the legal check clears.
