Step 3

Flexibility value compression, not spark spread alone.

W23 refinement, per operator response. TTF gas elevates above break-even for Dutch greenhouse CHP/WKK on the classical spark spread component. Spark-spread inversion is one component of WKK value rather than the whole picture. Imbalance market participation, emergency power provision, and flexibility revenue carry independent value layers that adjust separately based on grid conditions. Net effect on total flexibility value is the load-bearing question, not gas price alone.

Step 4

Operator-level response variance.

Response heterogeneity in the Naaldwijk cluster. Peakers absorb compressed spark spread by leaning on imbalance and flexibility revenue. Baseload-leaning operators step back, reduce dispatch hours, or idle units. Financially stressed operators accelerate decisions to reduce cultivated area or mothball glasshouse sections.

Step 5

Grid-level flexibility profile shift.

New W23 step, surfaced by the multi-component framing. Reduced CHP/WKK dispatch in Naaldwijk drops distributed generation feeding the Westland Infra medium-voltage grid, shifting regional feeder flow from a net-export profile to a net-import profile. Simultaneously reduces flexible capacity available to Westland Infra for congestion management and balancing. Qualitatively different challenge than a standard load-reduction scenario.

Step 6

The ACM consequence.

The congestion-profile shift plus reduced CHP-based flexibility forces alternative congestion-management procurement at higher cost, or reduced grid utilisation. The combined effect compresses the efficiency of regulated capital deployment and creates ACM cost-disallowance risk if congestion expenditure rises above the regulatory cost envelope without a tariff adjustment.
